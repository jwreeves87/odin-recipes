Smoked Baby Back Ribs

This recipe for smoked baby back ribs will yield ribs that are more competition style. That means they'll be stickier and sweeter than most. This also means they produce a tender bite of meat that pulls from the bone easily but the meat does not fall from the bone.

If you prefer ribs that are more tender, simply cook the ribs longer and they will continue to tenderize.

My preferred wood of choice for this smoke is Apple Wood, but use the one you like the best.

Logistics

  • Prep Time: 25 minutes
  • Cook Time: 5 hours
  • Smoker Temp: 225 Degrees
  • Meat Finish Temp: 190 Degrees
  • Cooking Style: Indirect heat

Ingredients

  • 1-3 Slabs of baby back ribs
  • Regular yellow mustard as a binder
  • 1-3 sticks of butter (1 per rack of ribs)
  • Brown Sugar
  • Honey
  • Your favorite dry rub
  • Your favorite barbecue sauce
  • Cooking Style: Indirect heat

Steps

  1. Prepare your ribs
  2. Get the Smoker Ready
  3. Smoke your Ribs
  4. Alternate Method for Super Tender Smoked Baby Back Ribs (2-2-1)
  5. Finish Up and Serve the Smoked Baby Back Ribs
