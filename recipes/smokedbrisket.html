Instructions

  1. 1 whole packer brisket (14-16 lbs, Choice+ grade)
  2. Coarse kosher salt (Diamond Crystal preferred)
  3. 16-mesh cracked black pepper (Tellicherry grade)
  4. Yellow mustard (French's classic - binding agent)
  5. Post oak wood chunks (seasoned 6+ months)
  6. Hickory wood chunks (20% blend ratio)
  7. Pink butcher paper (unbleached, food-grade)
  8. Probe thermometer (dual-probe recommended)
  9. Trimming: Square cut the point, remove hard fat, maintain 1/4" fat cap. Score fat cap in 1" crosshatch pattern
  10. Seasoning: Apply thin mustard layer, season with 50/50 salt/pepper ratio at 1 Tbsp per pound. Rest 2+ hours
  11. Smoker Setup: Preheat to 250°F ±5°F, establish clean-burning fire with 80/20 oak/hickory blend
  12. Initial Smoke: Place fat-side down, insert probe into thickest part of flat. Maintain 250°F, no opening for 6 hours
  13. Stall Management: At 165°F internal (6-8 hours), wrap in butcher paper. Monitor bark development
  14. Final Phase: Continue to 203°F internal, testing probe tenderness (should slide like butter)
  15. Rest Period: Hold wrapped at 145-165°F for 2-4 hours minimum (Cambro or cooler)
  16. Slicing: Separate point/flat, slice against grain at 1/4" thickness. Serve immediately
