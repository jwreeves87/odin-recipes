Texas-Style Smoked Brisket

About This Recipe

This Texas-style smoked brisket recipe delivers the authentic barbecue experience with a simple salt and pepper rub and low-and-slow smoking. The result is tender, juicy brisket with a beautiful smoke ring and that coveted bark.

Brisket is the holy grail of BBQ - it takes patience, but the results are absolutely worth it. This recipe focuses on letting the meat shine with minimal but effective seasoning.

For best results, use Oak or Hickory wood. The key is maintaining consistent temperature and being patient - don't rush the process!

Logistics

  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Cook Time: 12-16 hours
  • Smoker Temp: 250°F
  • Finish Temp: 203°F
  • Style: Indirect heat

Ingredients

  1. 1 whole packer brisket (12-15 lbs)
  2. Coarse kosher salt
  3. Coarse black pepper
  4. Garlic powder (optional)
  5. Yellow mustard (as binder)
  6. Oak or hickory wood chunks
  7. Aluminum foil or butcher paper

Instructions

  1. Trim excess fat, leaving about 1/4 inch fat cap
  2. Apply mustard binder and season generously with salt and pepper
  3. Let brisket rest at room temperature for 1 hour
  4. Preheat smoker to 250°F with wood chunks
  5. Smoke fat-side down until internal temp reaches 165°F (6-8 hours)
  6. Wrap in butcher paper or foil (Texas Crutch)
  7. Continue smoking until internal temp reaches 203°F
  8. Rest wrapped for 1-2 hours before slicing
  9. Slice against the grain and serve
